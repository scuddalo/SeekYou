DESIGN

The technology is the (more or less) easy part (although, in fairness, our CTO disagrees). What makes SeekYou really stand out is the relentless focus and attention to detail we've paid to four critical factors...

1: SeekYou is all-business.

This isn't Foursquare, Gowalla, or WeeConnect, we don't have cute little badges, you can't become the mayor of anything, and we don't fire off a tweet when you check in at Pierogie World, the House o' Blintzes, or Hair Today, Blonde Tomorrow.

We designed SeekYou to be a serious professional tool that that will help you maintain and deepen your professional connections.

2: Pre-formed Groups

Applications such as Loopt, Britekite, aki-aki, and Rummble are fine if you want to let your friends know that you've found a new bar or hooked up with an impromptu party over at Chloe's. And you can add individual friends, acquaintances, or people you don't even know to your database.

That's not SeekYou. SeekYou enables you to add the professional contacts that matter, as a group. Your graduate school class. Your alumni association. Your professional colleagues. Your clients. Your LinkedIn list. The point is that these are the people that you actually need (and want) to connect with, and SeekYou builds your database a group at a time.

3: Privacy

The number one concern everyone has about location-based services and social networks is privacy. Privacy, security, and controllable visibility were where we began when we began building SeekYou. We knew that connecting when you want, with whom you want, or being visible or invisible when you want would be critical. As a result, we created a structure of "tiered rings" to help you manage connections.

Think of it this way: inside Tier 1 is everyone that you absolutely want to be visible to, almost all the time (except when you want to be completely invisible): your family, key members of your team, key clients, key friends from school, and so on. Tier 2 might be colleagues from your office, a broader list of clients, your graduate school class. Tier 3 might include your LinkedIn list. And Tiers 4, 5, and, well, however many other tiers you want to create, include more and more people who are increasingly less intimately connected with you.

The point is that you create the tiers, you determine who goes where, you can be as visible or invisible as you want to be, to whomever you want, whenever you want.

4: Design

Our CEO used to be a big creative director at a big ad agency. One of our principal investors is a world-famous designer. Our CTO has designed hugely-popular games for a hugely-popular gaming company and high-profile software for a high-profile consumer electronics company. We've taken a passion for good design and ease of use and applied it to every aspect of SeekYou: the graphics, the user interface, the intuitive, predictive functionality, and everything else.

We wanted SeekYou to not only be useful, but be a joy to use.
